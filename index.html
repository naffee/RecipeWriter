Blog Inspiration Styles

Here are details of the recipe writing styles supported by this assistant, which have been modeled from the internet's most successful food blogs.

Pinch of Yum Style

Cozy, conversational, and highly personal storytelling.

  • The Hook: Personal anecdote or everyday family context (why they made it, how it felt).
  • Photography focus: Descriptions of textures, color, and steaming pans.
  • Structure: High-impact title, story introduction, "How to Make" visual steps summary, structured recipe card, and "Recipe FAQs".
  • Tone: Warm, friendly, approachable, written like a message to a friend.

Serious Eats Style

Science-based, rigorous culinary analysis and absolute clarity.

  • The Hook: Deconstructing the classic version of the dish and finding its flaws.
  • Testing Notes: Explanations of culinary chemistry (e.g. why salt draws out moisture, why reverse searing works).
  • Structure: Brief intro, "The Science of [Dish]", "Key Testing Steps", highly exact recipes with weight measurements (grams), and detailed equipment suggestions.
  • Tone: Enthusiastic, analytical, precise, authoritative.

Sally's Baking Addiction Style

Detailed baking blueprints with troubleshooting guides.

  • The Hook: Passionate baking enthusiasm and promise of foolproof results.
  • Baker's Cautions: "Why room temperature butter is critical", "Do not overmix", flour spooning warnings.
  • Structure: Enthusiastic introduction, "5 Keys to Success", "Ingredients Breakdown", step-by-step instructions with volume/metric weight toggles, and extensive storage/make-ahead tips.
  • Tone: Encouraging, educational, supportive, detail-oriented.
